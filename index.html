Out of Context

We Built the Future of Work. It Doesn’t Work.

“They wanted beer without barrels. Freedom without form. They got exactly what they asked for—nothing.”
—Klaus Brandl, Brewmaster

The Confession

In 2015, Dr. Thorsten Fuchs co-authored “Out of Office,” celebrating the digital transformation of work. A decade later, he and Anna-Sophie Fuchs admit: We got it catastrophically wrong.

From context collapse in Slack channels to organizations that measure everything except what matters, this book diagnoses why modern workplaces create exhaustion instead of excellence.

The Diagnosis

Drawing on mathematical theory, systems thinking, and the surprising wisdom of a Bavarian brewmaster, the authors reveal a startling truth:

Every organization broke the same way.

The Reconstruction

With brutal honesty and unexpected hope, OUT OF CONTEXT charts a path from boundary dissolution to meaningful reconstruction. Inside you’ll discover:

  • Why removing boundaries erased meaning
  • The mathematical laws of organizational failure
  • How to rebuild without more transformation theater
  • Why Klaus the brewmaster sees what consultants miss
  • Tools for Monday morning, not manifestos
